Study Techniques:

50/10 split:
- First 25 Minutes (Theory): Read your book (Hacking and Security) or watch a Network+ module. Focus purely on understanding the "why" and "how."
- Next 25 Minutes (Execution): Immediately open your virtual environment, Wireshark, or terminal and replicate what you just read. If the book talked about Nmap flags, run those exact scans on a target VM. Nothing cures phone addiction like an active terminal.
- 10 Minutes (The Break): Walk away from screens entirely. Stand up, stretch, grab water. Do not look at your phone during this break, or you will ruin your brain's dopamine reset.

Feynman Technique:
- You do not truly understand a security concept until you can explain it simply. After finishing a chapter on cryptography or routing protocols, pretend you are explaining it to a non-technical friend.
- Create a knowledge base of your own cheat sheets. If you learn a tricky command-line syntax or a specific network subnetting trick, write a quick guide for your future self.
